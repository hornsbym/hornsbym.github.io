Man of Stone

"I was a great man. Then I was dead. Now, I am stone. The city I founded passed the crown on to another as soon as they put me in the soil. Time does not stop, even when kings die."

Dialogue Story

The man climbed the three steps to his front porch. He noticed, through the frosted glass on his front door, that the kitchen lights were still on. Great, he thought to himself. No use trying to be quite. He opened his front door, not bothering to stifle the rattle of his keys against the door, and then let it close loudly. After hanging his cap on the wall next to the door, he made his way into the kitchen.
